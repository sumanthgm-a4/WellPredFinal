If a student consistently shows signs of withdrawal, lack of motivation, poor academic performance, and emotional instability over two weeks or more, they may be struggling with depression.

Signs to watch for:

  • Frequent complaints of headaches or stomach issues without a medical reason
  • Expressing feelings of being a burden
  • Declining grades or missing school
  • Talking about death or self-harm (seek help immediately!)

  • Maintain a consistent sleep schedule (7-9 hours per night)
  • Engage in physical activities and outdoor exercises
  • Stay connected with friends and family
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and proteins
  • Set realistic academic and personal goals
  • Seek counseling or therapy if needed
